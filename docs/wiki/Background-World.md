# Background World: The Confluence

> **Wiki page version:** EN 1.0.0 · **Product baseline:** v0.1.3 · **Updated:** 2026-07-18
> **中文:** [世界背景：汇流境](Background-World-zh-CN) · **Translation status:** synced with EN 1.0.0

The Confluence is an original frontier where trade roads, river basins, mountain passes,
islands, old ritual landscapes, and migrating communities overlap. Geography creates
constraints and opportunities; culture is not assigned from a color-coded biome.

## Design Pillars

1. **The road is political.** Maintained roads, ferries, bridges, passes, caravan
   stations, and seasonal closures determine where authority and supplies can travel.
2. **Water remembers.** Rivers support farms and towns, wetlands hide resources and
   disease, and flood/deposition history changes routes and claims.
3. **Knowledge is equipment.** Maps, remedies, scripts, contracts, engineering methods,
   and ritual tools unlock actions as meaningfully as weapons.
4. **Power has a cost.** Attacks, armor, techniques, difficult travel, and magic consume
   stamina, focus, load, supplies, exposure tolerance, time, or social trust.
5. **Cultures are plural.** Chinese historical influences inform material culture,
   medicine, roads, archives, administration, craft, and martial traditions without
   collapsing eras and regions into one fantasy stereotype.

## Geographic Regions

- **River leagues:** farms, ferry towns, levees, mills, boat guilds, archives of flood
  marks, and disputes over channels.
- **Pass domains:** forts, shrines, relay stations, mines, cliff paths, and communities
  whose survival depends on seasonal access.
- **Road confederacies:** market towns and caravan networks that negotiate maintenance,
  tolls, security, and supply rather than forming one uniform empire.
- **Island and coast polities:** ports, reef knowledge, shipyards, saltworks, fisheries,
  storm shelters, and contested straits.
- **Dryland routes:** oasis compacts, wells, mobile herding, astronomy, and strict water
  hospitality shaped by distance and heat.
- **Old landscapes:** ruins, ritual sites, altered watersheds, sealed roads, and material
  evidence that can be studied without making every mystery supernatural.

These are pressures, not fixed factions. A settlement can combine several and change as
roads, rivers, climate, trade, or conflict change.

## Institutions and Roles

Wayfinders, river wardens, pass sentinels, archive scholars, field physicians, craft
artisans, caravan couriers, and ritual adepts are occupations and social starting points,
not permanent classes. Future factions should declare geographic base, resource needs,
relationships, law/custom tags, and travel capabilities through content packs.

## Magic and Ritual

Magic is a registered capability system with visible costs and world interactions. It
does not bypass geography by default. A water technique may redirect a surface layer but
not silently rewrite a watershed; flight requires a capability and carries load, weather,
altitude, and exposure consequences; divination produces information with scope and
uncertainty rather than omniscient map revelation.

Ritual traditions are local, learned, and historically situated inside the fictional
world. They must not turn living Chinese religious or medical practices into generic
combat spell schools.

## Tone and Play

Combat is dangerous and deliberate, but the world is not only combat. Travel,
observation, negotiation, repair, gathering, shelter, trade, research, construction, and
local knowledge are valid play. The design borrows transparent tabletop reasoning and
committed action-game trade-offs, never protected lore, prose, art, or balance tables.
