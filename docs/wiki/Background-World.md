# Background World: The Confluence

The Confluence is an original frontier where trade roads, river basins, mountain passes,
islands, and old ritual landscapes overlap. Its cultures are shaped by geography and
exchange rather than mapped one-to-one onto a real dynasty or a fantasy franchise.

## Design Pillars

1. **The road is political.** Maintained roads, ferries, bridges, passes, and caravan
   stations determine where authority can travel.
2. **Water remembers.** Rivers support farms and towns, wetlands hide resources and
   disease, and seasonal floods rewrite routes.
3. **Knowledge is equipment.** Maps, local remedies, scripts, contracts, and ritual tools
   can unlock actions as meaningfully as weapons.
4. **Power has a cost.** Strong attacks, heavy armor, spells, and difficult travel consume
   stamina, focus, load, supplies, or exposure tolerance.
5. **Cultures are internally diverse.** Chinese historical influences appear in material
   culture, medicine, roads, bureaucracy, craft, and martial traditions without reducing
   them to a single elemental stereotype.

## Factions and Roles

The first data slice uses occupations instead of rigid classes: wayfinders, river
wardens, pass sentinels, archive scholars, field physicians, craft artisans, caravan
couriers, and ritual adepts. A background grants starting familiarity and social context;
it never prevents a character from learning another discipline.

## Tone

Combat may be dangerous and deliberate, but the world is not only combat. Travel,
observation, negotiation, repair, gathering, shelter, and local knowledge must remain
viable play. The project borrows transparent tabletop rules and committed action-game
trade-offs, not protected names, lore, art, text, or stat tables.
