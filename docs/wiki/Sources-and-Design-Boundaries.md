# Sources and Design Boundaries

> **Wiki page version:** EN 1.0.0 · **Product baseline:** v0.1.3 · **Updated:** 2026-07-18
> **中文:** [资料来源与设计边界](Sources-and-Design-Boundaries-zh-CN) · **Translation status:** synced with EN 1.0.0

The project synthesizes open rules, geography, and museum/history research while keeping
game content original, attributable, and culturally specific.

## Reference Families

- D&D System Reference Document 5.2.1 (CC BY 4.0): transparent abilities, checks,
  equipment requirements, and difficult-terrain concepts.
- Official Elden Ring player guides: public evidence for health/focus/stamina, equipment
  switching, requirements, scaling, and committed build trade-offs.
- Metropolitan Museum of Art and British Museum collections: object-level research for
  Chinese jian/dao forms, lacquered leather scale, iron lamellar, and ceremonial objects.
- UNESCO cultural material, including Dai medicine: examples of specific living
  traditions that prevent a monolithic “Chinese medicine” fantasy category.
- GIS and physical-geography references documented in `docs/GIS_FOUNDATIONS.md` for
  latitude, elevation, relief, climate, drainage, landforms, and settlement reasoning.

## Mandatory Boundaries

- Do not copy protected names, lore, prose, art, audio, maps, UI, animation frames, or
  balance tables from commercial games or franchises.
- Do not use Pokémon names/assets/audio as game content.
- Do not claim one object, dynasty, region, ethnicity, or living practice represents all
  Chinese history or culture.
- Identify source period, region, material, and uncertainty when a design relies on a
  specific historical reference.
- Label synthesis and inference as game design, not historical fact.
- Assets must be original/generated or verified CC0 and registered with provenance,
  modifications, license, and checksum.
- Short factual summaries may cite sources; substantial source-derived prose stays in
  research notes rather than executable JSON.

## Originality Review Questions

1. Could this name, silhouette, iconography, or mechanic be mistaken for one protected
   franchise rather than a broad genre convention?
2. Is a historical claim specific enough to verify and respectful of living traditions?
3. Does the design recombine sources into the Confluence rather than reskinning a real
   polity or commercial setting?
4. Are gameplay statistics and trade-offs original abstractions?
5. Is every committed asset licensed and machine-registered?

Direct URLs and longer research notes live in `docs/CHARACTER_SYSTEM_BLUEPRINT.md`,
`docs/GIS_FOUNDATIONS.md`, and asset attribution records. Update those sources before
making a Wiki claim more specific.
