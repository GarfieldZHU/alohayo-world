# Sources and Design Boundaries

The blueprint synthesizes public rules and museum/history references while keeping all
game content original.

## Reference Families

- Wizards of the Coast, _System Reference Document 5.2_ (CC BY 4.0): readable abilities,
  checks, equipment requirements, and difficult-terrain concepts.
- Bandai Namco official _Elden Ring_ guides: player-facing evidence for health/focus/
  stamina, equipment switching, requirements, scaling, and build experimentation.
- The Metropolitan Museum of Art and British Museum collection essays/objects: Chinese
  jian and dao forms, armor, lacquered leather scale, iron lamellar construction, and
  ceremonial equipment.
- UNESCO Courier material on Dai medicine: an example of a specific living Chinese
  medical tradition used to avoid treating "Chinese medicine" as one uniform fantasy
  magic system.

## Boundaries

- Do not copy protected game names, lore, prose, art, balance tables, or UI.
- Do not claim one historical object represents all periods or regions.
- Label inference as design synthesis, not historical fact.
- Keep translated content and citations separate from executable rules.
- Add provenance to assets and substantial source-derived text.

Repository research notes and direct URLs are maintained in
`docs/CHARACTER_SYSTEM_BLUEPRINT.md`.
